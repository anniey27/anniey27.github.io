Assignment 4: Libraries!

Overview

I created schematics (below) for two components: a capacitive sensor and a servo motor. For the capacitive sensor, it receives a signal through pin 2, which is routed through a 1 megaohm resistor to pin 4. When you touch a metal object (connected through an alligator clip), it triggers a change in capacitance, sending a signal back to the system that it can read from (which will decide when the servo will change positions). The 1 megaohm resistor is needed for absolute touch on the object to activate a signal. Meanwhile, the servo motor receives power and a pulse signal from pin 9, and connects to ground.

Building the Circuit

I brought the schematic to life, connecting my LEDs to pin 9 and 10. The joystick is on its own. It grabs power from 5V and connects to ground. VRx and VRy read from analog pins A0 and A1. Lastly, SW on the joystick is the switch, which functions by outputting a digital signal if the joystick is moved. This is connected to digital pin 3.
